Fighting Ghouls

Ghouls are AI enemies that become increasingly dangerous. Learn to identify and counter each type effectively.

Normal Ghouls

LOW THREAT

Characteristics:

  • Weak and slow moving
  • Start appearing after 2 in-game days
  • Easy to kill with basic weapons
  • Attack in small groups

Strategy:

Use stone pickaxe or metal tools. Keep distance and attack when they approach.

Fast Ghouls

MEDIUM THREAT

Characteristics:

  • Faster movement speed
  • Can quickly close distance
  • More aggressive behavior
  • Higher damage output

Strategy:

Use ranged weapons when possible. Kite them around obstacles and maintain distance.

Armored Ghouls

HIGH THREAT

Characteristics:

  • Heavily protected with armor
  • Require good weapons to damage
  • High health pools
  • Resistant to weak attacks

Strategy:

Use firearms or high-tier melee weapons. Focus fire and use teamwork when possible.

Explosive Ghouls

EXTREME THREAT

Characteristics:

  • Detonate on contact with players or structures
  • Massive damage to both player and base
  • Can destroy walls and equipment
  • Often approach in groups

Strategy:

Use long-range weapons exclusively. Never let them get close. Consider sacrificial walls.

General Ghoul Defense Tips

Wall Defense

Always use stone walls - wood walls are too weak. Repair bots are essential for automated defense.

Timing Preparation

Ghouls start appearing after 2 in-game days. Prepare defenses and weapons beforehand.

Team Fighting

Ghouls are much easier to handle with teammates. Coordinate attacks and share resources.

Escape Routes

Always have multiple escape routes planned. Don't get cornered by ghoul swarms.

PvP Combat Strategies

Player vs Player combat requires different tactics than fighting ghouls. Understand positioning, timing, and psychology.

Ranged Combat

Advantages:

  • Safe distance from enemy attacks
  • Better positioning control
  • Can engage multiple enemies
  • Effective against melee players

Key Tactics:

  • Kiting: Move while shooting to avoid return fire
  • High ground: Use elevation for better angles
  • Cover usage: Use walls and obstacles for protection
  • Ammo management: Don't waste shots on moving targets

Melee Combat

Advantages:

  • No ammo requirements
  • High damage at close range
  • Always available as backup
  • Good for ambush tactics

Key Tactics:

  • Ambush: Use buildings and terrain for surprise attacks
  • Circle strafing: Move around enemy while attacking
  • Timing: Attack right after enemy shoots
  • Retreat: Know when to disengage and heal

Advanced PvP Tips

Information Warfare

Knowledge is power. Scout enemy bases, learn their routines, and identify weak points before engaging.

Timing Attacks

Attack when enemies are distracted, low on resources, or during ghoul waves when they're vulnerable.

Psychological Warfare

Sometimes diplomacy works better than violence. Consider alliances, trades, and negotiations.

Hit and Run

Don't commit to prolonged fights. Strike quickly, deal damage, and retreat before reinforcements arrive.

Combat Positioning

Positioning is often more important than equipment. Master these positioning concepts for combat advantage.

High Ground Advantage

Elevated positions provide significant tactical advantages in combat situations.

Benefits:

  • Better visibility of approaching enemies
  • Improved shooting angles
  • Psychological advantage over opponents
  • Easier to defend single access points

Application:

Build watchtowers, use building rooftops, and control hilltops during engagements.

Cover Usage

Effective use of cover can mean the difference between life and death in firefights.

Types of Cover:

  • Hard Cover: Walls, buildings, rocks (blocks all damage)
  • Soft Cover: Trees, bushes (reduces visibility)
  • Mobile Cover: Moving between cover points
  • Created Cover: Walls you build during combat

Technique:

Peek out to shoot, immediately return to cover. Never stay exposed longer than necessary.

Movement Patterns

Unpredictable movement makes you harder to hit and gives you tactical flexibility.

Movement Types:

  • Strafing: Side-to-side movement while shooting
  • Zigzag: Unpredictable direction changes
  • Circle Strafing: Moving in circles around enemy
  • Jump Shots: Jumping while shooting (harder to hit)

Key Principle:

Never move in straight lines or predictable patterns. Always be moving or behind cover.

Combat Survival Tips

Health Management

  • Always carry healing items before engaging in combat
  • Know your health status at all times
  • Retreat when health is low - living to fight another day is better
  • Use food strategically to maintain health during long fights

Resource Management

  • Don't waste valuable ammo on weak targets
  • Prioritize weapon durability over minor damage
  • Keep backup weapons in case primary weapon breaks
  • Know when to save resources vs when to commit them

Tactical Awareness

  • Always have an escape route planned before engaging
  • Be aware of your surroundings and potential third parties
  • Don't get tunnel vision - watch for flanking attempts
  • Communication with teammates is essential for survival

Engagement Timing

  • Don't fight when you're at a disadvantage
  • Pick fights you can win or avoid fighting altogether
  • Consider the risk vs reward of each engagement
  • Sometimes retreating and regrouping is the best strategy

Common Combat Mistakes

Fighting Without Preparation

Engaging in combat without proper weapons, armor, or healing items leads to quick death.

Standing Still While Shooting

Static targets are easy to hit. Always move while in combat to avoid enemy fire.

Chasing Low Health Enemies

Pursuing fleeing enemies often leads you into traps or away from your safe zone.

Fighting Multiple Enemies

Avoid being outnumbered. Use terrain and tactics to fight enemies one at a time.

Ignoring Escape Routes

Getting cornered with no way out leads to certain death. Always plan your exits.

Wasting Ammunition

Missing shots or shooting at impossible targets wastes valuable resources.
